This page provides complete, real-world calibration walkthrough examples for various test equipment. Each example includes actual measurements, troubleshooting steps, and results from calibration sessions.

Philosophy: These examples demonstrate the bootstrap calibration approach - calibrating test equipment without requiring any pre-calibrated reference equipment. All methods use fundamental physical constants and freely available reference signals (WWV, GPS, AC mains).

Common Calibration Techniques

WWV Reception Tips

  • Best frequencies by time of day:
    • 10 MHz - Daytime (0800-1700 local)
    • 5 MHz - Night (1800-0700 local)
    • 15/20 MHz - Daytime only, depends on propagation
  • Antenna: 15-20 meter wire, East-West orientation
  • Receiver: RTL-SDR dongle + SDR software (GQRX, SDR#)
  • Signal processing: Narrow CW filter (200 Hz) centered on 1 kHz tone
GPS Lock Troubleshooting
  • Cold start: 10-15 minutes typical
  • Warm start: 30-60 seconds
  • Indoor reception: Position antenna near window, may need outdoor active antenna
  • Minimum satellites: 4 for 3D fix, 8+ for best accuracy
  • LED indicators: Blinking = searching, Solid = locked

Voltage Reference Stability

  • Warmup time: 30 minutes minimum, 60 minutes for best stability
  • Temperature coefficient: LM4040 is 100 ppm/°C, keep out of drafts
  • Load regulation: Use high-impedance voltmeter (>10 MΩ input)
  • Battery voltage: Maintain >8V for 9V battery systems

Tools and Materials

Common to All Calibrations

  • Digital multimeter (being calibrated or verified)
  • Oscilloscope (for timebase calibration)
  • RTL-SDR dongle ($25) + antenna wire
  • GPS module with 1PPS output ($10-15)
  • Precision voltage reference: LM4040-10.0 ($2)
  • Precision resistors: 0.1% tolerance ($1 each)
  • Breadboard or perfboard
  • 9V battery and holder
  • BNC connectors and cables

Recommended Test Equipment

  • Starting from scratch: Get a cheap DMM first (UNI-T UT61E, ~$60), calibrate it, use it to verify other equipment
  • Second purchase: DSO138 oscilloscope kit (~$20) for learning, or 1013D Plus (~$300) for serious work
  • RF work: NanoVNA ($50) is essential, TinySA ($100) is extremely useful
